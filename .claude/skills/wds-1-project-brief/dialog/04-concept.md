### Structural sequence (corrected)
1. Submit anonymously
2. Submissions revealed for listening (still anonymous)
3. Vote — submitters still anonymous
4. Voting closes — submitters revealed, scores shown, playlist generated

## Core Structural Idea

**Anonymity-through-judgment.** Submissions are anonymous from submission through voting completion. The reveal is the social moment — it comes after all evaluations are locked in, so voting is purely about the music.

## Why This Approach

Removes social bias ("I always vote for Alex's picks"), keeps voting honest, and makes the reveal genuinely fun — you find out who picked what only after you've already committed your votes.

## Concrete Example

You hear a track you love during a tape. You vote for it based purely on how it hits. After voting closes, you discover your friend who "only listens to metal" submitted a tender folk song. That surprise is the product's signature moment.

## Features That Stem From This

- Hidden submissions until listening phase
- Anonymous listening/voting phase
- Locked votes (no changing after reveal)
- Post-reveal submitter attribution
- Post-reveal comments and discussion
- Playlist generated at close as the shared artifact
