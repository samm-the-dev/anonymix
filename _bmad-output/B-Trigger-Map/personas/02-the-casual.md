# The Casual — Priority 2 (SECONDARY)

> The drop-in player who keeps the group at critical mass

**Document:** Trigger Map - Secondary Persona
**Created:** 2026-03-24
**Status:** COMPLETE

---

## Profile Summary

The Casual enjoys the game but doesn't organize their week around it. They submit most rounds, skip some, vote when it's convenient, and occasionally drop a comment. They're not disengaged — they genuinely like the concept — but they have a lower activation energy threshold. If something is even slightly annoying (confusing UI, too many steps, ads), they'll just... not do it this week. And next week. And then they've quietly stopped playing.

The Casual is the participation rate. In a group of 10, there might be 3-4 Enthusiasts and 5-6 Casuals. Keeping Casuals engaged is what keeps rounds feeling full and diverse.

---

## Who The Casual Is

Same age range and friend group as The Enthusiast, but music is a smaller part of their identity. They listen daily but don't think of themselves as "music people." They have a go-to playlist they shuffle, discover songs mostly through algorithms or what friends post, and rarely seek out new music actively. The game format gives them a fun reason to participate that they wouldn't create on their own.

---

## Psychological Profile

The Casual values **ease and belonging**. They participate because it's a thing their friends do together, and they want to be part of it. The music is secondary to the social glue. They enjoy the prompt format because it gives them a **clear, bounded task** — find one song, submit it, done. Open-ended "share music whenever" doesn't work for them because there's no structure to participate in.

They have a **low tolerance for friction**. Not because they're impatient, but because the game isn't high enough on their priority list to fight through obstacles. Every unnecessary step is a reason to postpone, and postponing becomes skipping. They need the path from "I should submit" to "submitted" to be as short as possible.

They're **socially motivated but passively so**. They like seeing what others picked. They enjoy the reveal. They might not comment on every song, but they read the comments. The playlist is something they'll add to their rotation if it's easy to access.

---

## Internal State

When a prompt drops, The Casual feels a mild **"oh yeah, I should do that"** — not urgency, but pleasant recognition. If submission is quick and easy, they'll do it in the moment. If it requires effort (opening a separate app, dealing with auth issues, navigating ads), they'll think "I'll do it later" — and later often doesn't come.

During voting, they feel **light enjoyment** — it's fun to listen to the picks. But if the voting interface is cumbersome, they'll vote on a few and bail. The reveal gives them a **social buzz** — they like the "oh, of course [friend] picked that" moment.

Their dominant negative emotion is **quiet drift** — not frustration, just gradual disengagement when the cost of participation exceeds its casual reward.

---

## Usage Context

**Access/Discovery:** Invited by a friend, joins via link. Returns when prompted by group chat or notification that a new tape dropped.

**Emotional State:** Mildly positive when engaging, neutral otherwise. Not stressed, not excited — just participating in a group thing.

**Behavior Pattern:** Submits within 24 hours of deadline (not immediately). Votes in one sitting, doesn't revisit. Reads some comments, rarely writes them. Checks reveal results. Might save the playlist if it's one tap.

**Decision Criteria:** Is this quick? Can I do it from my phone in 2 minutes? Will I feel part of the group?

**Success Outcome:** They submitted, voted, saw the results, and it took less than 5 minutes total across the whole round. They feel included.

---

## Relationship to Business Goals

- ✅ **Active Participation (PRIMARY):** Makes up the majority of any group. Without Casuals, rounds feel empty and Enthusiasts lose motivation.
- ✅ **Engagement Quality:** Their participation adds diversity to submissions and votes. Even light engagement contributes to playlist quality.
- ✅ **Organic Growth:** Less likely to invite others actively, but if the app is easy, they won't resist when someone else invites them to a second session.

---

## Driving Forces

### WANTS
- ✅ **Participate without effort** — submit a song and vote without it becoming a task on their to-do list (when a tape is active)
- ✅ **Feel included in the group activity** — be part of the social ritual without needing to be the most engaged person (throughout the round)
- ✅ **Get a good playlist out of it** — walk away with new music to listen to without having to actively seek it out (when the tape closes)

### FEARS
- ❌ **Too many steps to participate** — if submitting requires more than a search + tap, they'll postpone indefinitely (when trying to submit)
- ❌ **Falling behind and feeling guilty** — missing rounds and feeling like they're letting the group down or becoming "the person who never plays" (when life gets busy)
- ❌ **Being judged for their music taste** — not being a "music person" and feeling like their picks will be seen as basic or uninspired (when choosing what to submit)

---

### Anonymix Promise (Wants):
- **Effortless participation:** Search, tap, submit. Vote with a tap. No ads between steps, no platform switching.
- **Social inclusion:** Notifications when tapes drop, clear deadlines, low-pressure format
- **Playlist artifact:** Auto-generated playlist on their platform (Spotify or YouTube Music) — new music delivered, no effort required

### Anonymix Answer (Fears):
- **Minimal steps:** Streamlined submission and voting — designed for the phone-in-hand, 2-minute interaction
- **No shame in skipping:** No aggregate leaderboard, no "participation streak" guilt mechanics. Miss a round, join the next one.
- **Anonymous submissions:** Their pick is anonymous through voting — no one knows it's theirs until after votes are locked. Safe to pick anything.
