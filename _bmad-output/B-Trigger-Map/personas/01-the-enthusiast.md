# The Enthusiast — Priority 1 (PRIMARY)

> The core player who makes each round worth playing

**Document:** Trigger Map - Primary Persona
**Created:** 2026-03-24
**Status:** COMPLETE

---

## Profile Summary

The Enthusiast is the heartbeat of any Anonymix session. They're the person who sees the prompt drop and immediately starts thinking about the perfect song. They don't just submit — they agonize over their pick, listen to everyone else's submissions with genuine curiosity, write comments that go beyond "nice pick," and actually listen to the generated playlist afterward. They're not trying to win. They're trying to share something that resonates.

They exist in every friend group that plays Music League. They're the reason rounds feel alive instead of perfunctory.

---

## Who The Enthusiast Is

Late 20s to late 30s, part of a tight friend group that shares interests beyond just music — but music is one of the connective threads. Uses either Spotify or YouTube Music daily. Has strong opinions about music but isn't snobby about it. Thinks of songs in terms of memories, moods, and contexts — not genres or technical merit. The kind of person who makes playlists for road trips, breakups, and seasons.

---

## Psychological Profile

The Enthusiast values **connection through curation**. For them, picking a song for a prompt is a creative act — it says something about who they are and how they see the world. They treat each tape like a small gift: "I heard this and I think you should hear it too."

They have a **collector's instinct** for music. Not in a completionist way, but in a "I'm always absorbing" way. They discover songs through friends, algorithms, films, and random encounters. Anonymix gives them a structured outlet for that instinct — a reason to share and a container for the conversation.

They're **socially attentive but not performative**. They notice when someone submits a deep cut and want to know the story behind it. They engage with the round as a social experience, not just a game mechanic. The reveal — finding out who picked what — is their favorite moment.

Competition makes them slightly uncomfortable when it dominates. They'll participate in competitive formats, but they disengage when scoring feels like the point. **Resonance** as a frame speaks directly to them.

---

## Internal State

When a new tape prompt drops, The Enthusiast feels a spark of **creative excitement** — "Oh, what would I pick for this?" There's a pleasant tension in curating the perfect submission. During voting, they feel **genuine curiosity** about what others chose and why. The reveal triggers **social warmth** — delight when a friend's pick surprises them, satisfaction when their own pick resonated.

When the experience has friction — ads interrupting listening, platform lock-outs, stalled rounds — they feel **low-key frustration** that the tool is getting in the way of the thing they actually care about: the music and the people.

---

## Usage Context

**Access/Discovery:** Already in the friend group. Gets invited via link or in-group chat. Returns via PWA bookmark or home screen shortcut.

**Emotional State:** Excited when a prompt drops. Curious during listening/voting. Warm during reveal. Mildly annoyed by any friction that slows them down.

**Behavior Pattern:** Engages deeply but on their own schedule. Might submit quickly if inspiration strikes, or wait until the last day to finalize. Listens to all submissions, not just a few. Reads and writes comments. Checks back for the reveal.

**Decision Criteria:** Is this fun? Is this easy? Do I get to discover new music and talk about it with my friends?

**Success Outcome:** They finish a round feeling like they shared something and discovered something. The playlist gets saved. The group chat lights up during the reveal.

## Relationship to Business Goals

- ✅ **Active Participation (PRIMARY):** IS the engine. Their consistent engagement is what makes rounds feel alive and worth coming back to. Without enthusiasts, sessions die.
- ✅ **Engagement Quality:** Drives comment depth, playlist value, and round momentum. Their engagement sets the tone for everyone else.
- ✅ **Organic Growth:** Most likely to say "we should get [friend] to join" and to host their own sessions eventually.

---

## Driving Forces

### WANTS
- ✅ **Discover music through trusted friends** — hear things they'd never find on their own, filtered through people whose taste they respect (when browsing submissions each round)
- ✅ **Express identity through curation** — the prompt-and-pick format lets them say something about themselves without having to explain it (when choosing their submission)
- ✅ **Experience the social moment of the reveal** — the surprise and delight of finding out who picked what, and why (when votes close and attributions appear)

### FEARS
- ❌ **Friction killing the vibe** — ads, login failures, clunky UI turning a fun social ritual into a chore (every time they open the app)
- ❌ **Competition overshadowing connection** — scores becoming the point instead of the music, turning friends into opponents (when viewing results)
- ❌ **Being excluded by platform** — wanting to participate but being locked out because they use YouTube Music instead of Spotify (at signup)

---

### Anonymix Promise (Wants):
- **Discovery through friends:** Small trusted groups, anonymous voting removes bias, every submission gets a fair listen
- **Identity through curation:** Custom prompts, one song per tape, the pick IS the statement
- **The reveal moment:** Built into the game loop — anonymous through judgment, attributed after

### Anonymix Answer (Fears):
- **No friction:** Zero ads, clean PWA, no auth loops
- **Resonance over ranking:** No aggregate leaderboard, voting framed as resonance, per-tape only
- **Platform-inclusive:** Spotify and YouTube Music both first-class — no one gets left out
