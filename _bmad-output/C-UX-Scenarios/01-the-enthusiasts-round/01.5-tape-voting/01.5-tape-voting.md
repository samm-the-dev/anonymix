## Overview

**Page Purpose:** Comment on songs that resonated with you. The core feedback mechanism — expressive enough to spark conversation, low-pressure enough for the couch. No points, no ranking. Just share what hit you.

**Entry Context:** User has listened to the playlist in their music app. Returns to Anonymix via notification ("Time to comment!") or sees updated tape card status in session view.

**Exit Action:** Submits comments → returns to session view. Tape moves to reveal phase when the commenting deadline passes.

## Design Decisions

- **Comments-only feedback:** No points, no ranking. Comments are the signal. If a song moved you, say something. If not, no worries — skip it.
- **Comment field per song:** Each song gets a comment field. Can be a sentence, a word, an emoji, or left blank. Save draft on blur.
- **"The Tape" comment:** An optional overall comment field at the top or bottom — react to the tape as a whole, not just individual songs.
- **"No worries" framing:** The UI communicates that commenting is optional per song. No guilt, no minimum. The absence of a comment is not a slight — it just means you didn't have something to say about that one.
- **Can't comment on own submission:** User's own song excluded from the commenting list (or shown but comment field disabled).
- **Anonymous submissions:** Songs are still anonymous — no submitter names visible during commenting.

## Content Needs

- List of submissions: song title, artist, album art (anonymous — no submitter names)
- Per-song: comment field (optional, save draft on blur)
- "The Tape" overall comment field (optional)
- Submit comments button
- User's own submission not shown in commenting list (or comment-disabled)

## Driving Forces Addressed

- **Social discovery (14-15):** Reacting to what friends picked, building anticipation for the reveal
- **Self-expression:** Comments are taste signaling — what you choose to say (or not say) about a song reflects your relationship with the music
- **Low friction (14-15):** No points to agonize over. Just listen and react naturally.

## On-Page Interactions (Storyboard Items)

- Submissions displayed anonymously (no submitter names until reveal)
- Open question: commenting deadline — fixed window, or closes when host triggers reveal?
