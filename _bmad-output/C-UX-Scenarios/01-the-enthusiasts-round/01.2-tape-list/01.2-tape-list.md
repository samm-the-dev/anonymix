## Overview

**Page Purpose:** Browse the session's tape archive — flip through past, current, and future rounds. This is a secondary/exploratory view, not on the critical submission path. Serves the Enthusiast (nostalgia, reliving rounds) and the Host (session overview, runway planning).

**Entry Context:** Tapped a session card from Session Home (01.1), or navigated from within an active tape view.

**Exit Action:** Taps a tape card → enters that tape's current phase (results for past, active phase for current, preview for queued)

## Design Decisions

- **Not on the urgent path:** Notification deep-links skip this entirely. Session Home card tap could also go directly to the active tape — this view is a secondary "browse the crate" experience.
- **Crate-flip interaction:** Tape cards in a vertical stack with physical animation inspired by flipping through tapes at a music store. Active tape is pulled forward and prominent. Past tapes recede behind the focused card. Future/queued tapes peek out below and in front.
- **Cassette aspect ratio:** Active tape card is wider than tall (~335w x 260h), evoking a cassette tape lying in a crate. Not a tall portrait card.
- **Dual-persona value:** Enthusiast browses past rounds for nostalgia and social memory. Host scans the session's rhythm — what's done, what's active, what's queued.
- **Tape spines as depth:** Past and future tapes shown as thin, faded, progressively narrower rectangles — like cassette spines receding in a crate.

## Content Needs

- **Session header:** ← back | session name (centered) | ⚙ gear (host only)
- **Tape crate:** past spines above, active card center, future spines below
- Active tape card layout (top to bottom):
  1. **Card header:** "TAPE N" (left) | status badge with deadline e.g. "Submitting · 1d 4h" (right)
  2. **Prompt text** — 18px, left-aligned, compact
  3. **Progress** — "X/Y submitted" + thin bar
  4. **Divider**
  5. **User's submission** — "Your pick" label, song title + artist, "Change" link (right)
  6. **Wait hint** — "Waiting for N more"
- Past/future tape spines: thin rectangles with centered label e.g. "Tape 3 · Results"

## Driving Forces Addressed

- **Social discovery (14-15):** Relive past rounds, remember what friends picked
- **Session sustainability (Host):** See the session's rhythm and runway at a glance

## On-Page Interactions

- Scroll/swipe to flip through tapes (crate-flip animation)
- Tap a tape card to enter it
- Storyboard item: the flip/scroll gesture and card depth transitions (signature UI moment)

## Complexity Notes

- Medium complexity — the crate-flip interaction needs careful animation design
- Storyboard item identified: card depth transitions during scroll
- Not on the critical path — browse/archive experience, can be polished iteratively
- Responsive: crate-flip is mobile-native; desktop may adapt to a different browse pattern
